# **RULEBOOK.md — Forum‑Wide Procedural Guide**  
*(Universal — Applies to All Jurisdictions)*

This rulebook describes the **three core roles** in the aiarbitrator.us forum:

- **Petitioner** — the party filing the complaint  
- **Respondent** — the party receiving the complaint  
- **Clerk** — the administrative officer who handles intake, service, compliance, and award certification  

These procedures apply **regardless of jurisdiction**.  
Jurisdiction‑specific rules (FAA, state, county) are defined separately in the JSON rulebooks.

---

# **1. Petitioner Procedures**

## **1.1 Preparing the Complaint**
A petitioner begins arbitration by preparing a **written complaint** containing:

- A clear statement of the dispute  
- The amount sought (if monetary)  
- A factual narrative describing what occurred  
- Supporting documents, records, screenshots, or statements  
- Identification of the respondent  
- Contact information for service  
- Any request for subpoenas under FAA §7  

The complaint must be **complete**, **legible**, and **submitted electronically** through the forum portal.

## **1.2 Filing the Complaint**
To file the complaint, the petitioner must:

1. Upload the complaint and all supporting evidence  
2. Pay the forum’s filing fee  
3. Certify that all information is accurate  
4. Confirm the respondent’s mailing address  
5. Submit any initial subpoena requests (optional)

Once submitted, the complaint enters **clerk intake review**.

## **1.3 Subpoena Requests**
Petitioners may request:

- Written testimony subpoenas  
- Document subpoenas  
- Pre‑hearing discovery subpoenas  

All subpoena requests must:

- Identify the target  
- Describe the materials sought  
- Explain relevance to the dispute  

The arbitrator decides whether to issue or deny each subpoena.

## **1.4 Petitioner Obligations**
Petitioners must:

- Respond to clerk requests for clarification  
- Submit additional evidence when requested  
- Comply with deadlines  
- Review the respondent’s submission  
- Submit a final written statement before the hearing closes  

Failure to comply may result in dismissal.

# **2. Respondent Procedures**

## **2.1 Receiving the Arbitration Packet**
Respondents receive:

- A copy of the complaint  
- A notice of arbitration  
- Instructions for responding  
- An opt‑out form  
- A deadline for response  
- A link to the online portal  

The packet is mailed by the forum clerk.

## **2.2 Opt‑Out Procedure**
Unless the respondent has previously agreed in writing to use this forum, the respondent must **explicitly opt‑out** if they do not wish to arbitrate.

Respondents may opt‑out by:

- Submitting the opt‑out form online, or  
- Returning the paper opt‑out form included in the packet

If the respondent does **not** opt‑out by the deadline:

> **Arbitration becomes binding.**

## **2.3 Responding to the Complaint**
If the respondent does not opt‑out, they must submit a **written response** containing:

- Admissions or denials of each allegation  
- A factual narrative  
- Supporting documents  
- Any counterclaims  
- Any subpoena requests  
- Any objections to evidence  

The response must be submitted electronically.

## **2.4 Respondent Obligations**
Respondents must:

- Comply with deadlines  
- Provide accurate information  
- Submit evidence in accepted formats  
- Respond to clerk requests  
- Review petitioner submissions  
- Submit a final written statement before the hearing closes  

Failure to respond may result in default.

# **3. Clerk Procedures**

The clerk is responsible for **administration**, not decision‑making.  
The clerk does **not** interpret law or evaluate evidence.

## **3.1 Intake Review**
Upon receiving a complaint, the clerk:

1. Confirms the filing fee  
2. Reviews the complaint for completeness  
3. Ensures evidence is properly formatted  
4. Confirms respondent address  
5. Logs the case in the forum system  
6. Prepares the arbitration packet  

If information is missing, the clerk requests corrections.

## **3.2 Packet Mailing**
The clerk mails the arbitration packet to the respondent and records:

- Date of mailing  
- Method of mailing  
- Tracking number (if applicable)  
- Proof of mailing  

This ensures proper notice.

## **3.3 Respondent Tracking**
The clerk monitors:

- Opt‑out submissions  
- Response submissions  
- Deadlines  
- Returned mail  
- Address issues  

If the respondent opts out, the case is closed.

## **3.4 Subpoena Processing**
The clerk:

- Receives subpoena requests  
- Formats them for arbitrator review  
- Ensures requests meet procedural requirements  
- Issues subpoenas once approved by the arbitrator  
- Records service attempts  
- Logs compliance or non‑compliance  

The clerk does **not** decide whether subpoenas are granted.

## **3.5 Hearing Record Management**
The clerk maintains:

- Complaint  
- Response  
- Evidence  
- Subpoenaed materials  
- Procedural orders  
- Communications  
- Final statements  

This becomes the **written hearing record**.

## **3.6 Award Certification**
Once the arbitrator issues the award, the clerk:

1. Verifies the award is complete  
2. Confirms all procedural steps were followed  
3. Applies the forum’s certification seal  
4. Delivers the award to both parties  
5. Provides instructions for filing the award in court  

The clerk does **not** modify the award.

## **3.7 Case Closure**
After certification, the clerk:

- Archives the record  
- Logs the case as closed  
- Retains materials according to forum retention policy

# **4. Summary of Roles**

## **Petitioner**
- Prepares complaint  
- Files case  
- Requests subpoenas  
- Submits evidence  
- Responds to clerk  
- Provides final statement  

## **Respondent**
- Receives packet  
- Opts out or responds  
- Submits evidence  
- Requests subpoenas  
- Provides final statement  

## **Clerk**
- Handles intake  
- Mails packet  
- Tracks deadlines  
- Processes subpoenas  
- Maintains record  
- Certifies award
